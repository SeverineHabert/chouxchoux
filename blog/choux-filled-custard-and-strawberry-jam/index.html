Choux filled Custard and Strawberry Jam

 Posted on June 30, 2018  •  Other languages: fr

Informations

Portions 16 choux
Difficulty Difficult
Cost Cheap
Total Time 45 min (overnight cooling)
Preparation Time 1 hour
Cooking Time 10 min (custard) + 20 min (choux)

 

A delicious little treat that is easy as long as we have time to prepare it in advance. This is often the case in French baking!

Adapted from Felder

 

#Pastry #French #Custard #Classics #Strawberry

Ingredients

  1. Craquelin:
  2. 50 g soft butter
  3. 60 g brown sugar
  4. 60 g flour
  5. few drop of food coloring
  6. Choux dough:
  7. 150 g flour
  8. 150 g milk
  9. 100 g water
  10. 1 tablespoon of sugar
  11. 80 g butter
  12. 5 eggs
  13. Custard:
  14. 500 mL whole milk
  15. 120 g sugar
  16. 50 g cornstarch
  17. 4 egg yolk (120g)
  18. vanilla pod
  19. 50 g butter
  20. Strawberry Jam Jar

Instructions

  1. Craquelin: The day before, soften the butter with a whisk, add the brown sugar, the flour and the food coloring and whisk again to mix. In between 2 baking paper sheets, roll the preparation into a thin layer of 3mm. Let freeze overnight in the freezer.
  2. Custard: In a mixing bowl, whisk half of the sugar with the cornstarch, add the egg yolks and whisk strongly (for a couple of seconds).
  3. Cut the vanilla pod in the length and extract all the vanilla grains and add them to the milk.
  4. Pour the (vanilla) milk, the rest of the sugar and the open vanilla pod into a cooking pot, whisk and bring to boil. Pour the hot milk onto the egg preparation and whisk.
  5. Pour the preparation back to the cooking pan, cook at middle heat while whisking. When the custard is setting, withdraw from the heat and add the butter.
  6. On a baking sheet, spread the custard and film with plastic wrap at the contact of the dough to avoid any crust to appear on the dough. Cool down in the fridge overnight.
  7. Choux: Preheat the oven at 200$^{\circ}$C (static heat). In a big cooking pot, heat the milk, the water, the sugar and the butter. At the first simmer, withdraw from the heat and the flour. Mix vigorously with a spatula. Bring back the pot to middle heat and keep mixing until the dough is homogeneous and does not stick to the pot´s side.
  8. Move the dough to a mixing bowl and incorporate the egg by one by one. The egg should completely absorbed before adding the next one. The texture at the end should be such as the dough falls in V-shape when lifting the spatula full of dough over the bowl.
  9. Pipe with a piping bag 2cm small dough balls on a baking sheet (covered with silicon mat). Leave several centimeters in between as they will rise a lot.
  10. Get the craquelin out of the freezer and cut 2 cm disk in the craquelin to add on top of the choux.
  11. Bake the choux for 20 min, do NOT open the oven door while baking.
  12. Final Dressing: Let cool down and then pipe inside (from the bottom) first a bit of strawberry jam and then the custard until the choux is full. Voilà, Bon Appétit!

✎ The craquelin is the trick to obtain perfect choux every time, and it gives a very nice look too!
